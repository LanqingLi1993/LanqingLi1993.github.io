I am currently a senior research scientist at Tencent AI Lab, where I work on machine learning and its applications in drug discovery and autonomous control. Prior to that, I was a Tech Lead and machine learning engineer at InferVision, developing computer-aided detection (CAD) softwares.

I hold a Master's Degree in Physics (PhD program) from The University of Chicago and received my Bechelor's Degree in Physics from MIT in 2015, where I was advised by Prof. Alan Guth, Prof. David Kaiser and Prof. Nevin Weinberg to conduct research in theoretical physics.

At Tencent I've worked on iDrug and iGrow solutions. I'm enthusiastic about artificial intelligence and its prospect of making a better world.

Research

I'm interested in deep learning, reinforcement learning (RL), representation learning and AI-aided drug discovery (AIDD). In particular, I work on the understanding and development of machine learning algorithms that are robust to distribution shift, offline data, noise and adversarial attacks. For those keen visitors, please find my related publications below.

Internship: I provide intermittent research internship opportunities preferably for students pursuing a PhD in machine learning and other related fields. Ideal candidates should be self-driven and highly motivated to work on the following projects:

  • Offline RL, Meta/Multi-task RL, Physics-informed RL
  • Robust Deep Learning, e.g., OOD/Long-tailed/Continual Learning
  • Physics-informed Deep Learning, Graph Neural Networks for Drug Discovery

Students who are interested in working with me are welcome to send an email to lanqingli@tencent.com.
